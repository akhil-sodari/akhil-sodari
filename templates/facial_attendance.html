A Novel Cost-Efficient System for Autonomous Attendance in Classroom Environments

Akhil Sodari


This project is my final year major project and is the result of my amazing four years at the Mahatma Gandhi Institute of Technology. I always aimed to bring some innovation to our awesome CSE Department. With this project, I wanted to tackle a real-world issue using technology.

In classrooms globally, manual attendance tracking eats up valuable time and frequently results in errors and inefficiencies. Furthermore, traditional biometric systems pose challenges like long queues and hefty costs. Back in 2021, facial recognition technology for attendance was prohibitively expensive.

To address these issues, I spearheaded a project focused on autonomous attendance management using facial recognition technology. By leveraging Viola-Jones and Linear Binary Patterns of Histograms algorithms, my system achieves an impressive 95.83% accuracy.

Eliminating the need for costly hardware, my solution relies instead on a simple smartphone to capture a video stream of students in the classroom. The result? An efficient, robust, and cost-effective approach to attendance tracking.

Key features of my system include:

  • Input: Video stream of students from a smartphone.
  • Algorithms: Viola-Jones and LBPH.
  • Accuracy: 95.83%.
  • Output: Excel sheet with names and roll numbers of present students.

With this project, I aimed to streamline attendance management, enhancing productivity and efficiency in educational institutions.
